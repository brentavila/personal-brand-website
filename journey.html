My Journey

How I got here—and why I'm still all in.

I didn't set out to become "the ads and automations guy." I set out to understand why some brands grew and others stalled—and to build the systems that make growth repeatable. Here's the path that got me here.

Where it started

Early on, I was less interested in marketing as a department and more in the mechanics: what actually moved people from stranger to customer, and how you could measure it. That curiosity led me into paid channels—where you could see, in real time, what worked and what didn't. No guessing. Just data, tests, and iteration.

I spent years running campaigns across search, social, and programmatic. I learned that scaling spend without a clear system is a fast way to burn budget. I also learned that the brands that won weren't the ones with the biggest wallets—they were the ones that had clean funnels, clear attribution, and a discipline for testing. That combination became my focus.

The shift to systems

Running campaigns taught me that ads are only one piece. The real leverage came when I started connecting the dots: landing pages, CRM, email sequences, and reporting. I got deep into marketing automations—Zapier, Make, CRM workflows—so that once a lead came in, the rest of the journey could run without manual handoffs.

That's when the "aha" hit: growth isn't just about getting more clicks. It's about turning those clicks into a predictable system. Audiences, creatives, and automations had to work together. I started designing full-funnel flows and building the reporting to see whether we were actually moving the needle. Over time, that systems mindset became the core of how I work.

Where I am today

Today I've launched over 1,300 campaigns and worked across 10+ platforms and tools. I've spent 8+ years in the trenches—testing audiences, fixing attribution, and building automations that actually convert. The work I do now sits at the intersection of paid media, funnel strategy, and ops: making sure the right message reaches the right person at the right time, and that the data behind it is clean enough to act on.

I care about ROAS, but I care just as much about incrementality and long-term value. I'd rather scale slowly with proof than blow budget on hope. That approach has stuck because it works—for e-commerce, lead gen, and everything in between.

Why I'm still obsessed

What keeps me in this work isn't the platforms or the tech—it's the outcome. There's something deeply satisfying about seeing a brand go from "we're not sure what's working" to "we know exactly which campaigns and flows drive revenue." I love turning messy spreadsheets and gut feelings into clear dashboards and repeatable playbooks.

I also love that the field never stands still. New channels, new attribution models, new automation tools—there's always something to learn and apply. That constant evolution is what makes the job interesting. I'm not here to do the same thing I did last year; I'm here to help brands grow in a way that's sustainable and measurable.

What I bring to the table

When you work with me, you get someone who thinks in systems, not one-off campaigns. I'll push on your funnel, your tracking, and your automation setup—because those are what make paid spend pay off. I'll also be straight with you: I'll tell you when to scale, when to hold, and when to fix the foundation first.

If you're looking for a partner who combines hands-on execution with a clear view of how growth actually works, I'd love to connect. Let's build something that scales.
